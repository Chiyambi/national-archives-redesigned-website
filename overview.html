National Archives of Malawi — Safeguarding the Nation's Memory

Introduction

The National Archives of Malawi is a government Department within the Ministry of Sports and Culture. Established in 1947 in Zomba as a regional branch of the then Central African Archives (later known as the National Archives of Rhodesia and Nyasaland), the institution has evolved significantly over the decades.

Following the dissolution of the Federation of Rhodesia and Nyasaland, the Zomba branch was formally declared the National Archives of Malawi on January 1, 1964—marking the beginning of an independent national archival institution dedicated to preserving Malawi's documentary heritage.

Mission Statement

"To collect, organize, control, preserve and make available to the Malawi Nation, and other interested persons, information which reflects the country's historical, political, judiciary, religious, economic, social and cultural heritage, irrespective of the media."

This mission statement encapsulates the Archives' commitment to being the custodian of Malawi's memory, ensuring that records of enduring value are preserved and accessible for current and future generations.

Core Functions

The National Archives of Malawi carries out its functions under two statutory obligations:

  • 1. The National Archives Act
  • 2. The Printed Publications Act

The administration of these functions is organized through multiple specialized sections:

Administration

Oversees day-to-day administrative operations and department management.

Records Centers

Three regional centers (Zomba, Lilongwe, Mzuzu) managing record transfer, retirement, and disposal with systematic registry practices.

Public Archives

Responsible for permanent preservation of records deemed of enduring historical value.

Historical Manuscripts

Collects and preserves papers from institutions and individuals who shaped Malawi's development.

Library

Develops comprehensive literature collections and administers ISBN issuance to Malawian publishers.

Conservation Centre

Handles document binding, microfilming, and photo-processing to enhance document lifespan.

Services Available

The National Archives of Malawi provides a comprehensive range of services to government, researchers, and the public:

  • Operation of a nation-wide records management system with low-cost accommodation
  • Advisory services on proper records management practices to government and organizations
  • Authorized access to public archives and historical manuscripts for researchers and scholars
  • Reference services and bibliographic research support
  • ISBN issuance and publishing advisory services
  • Conservation services including binding, microfilming, and document preservation

Access to Services

Everyone is welcome to access Archives services, though access is subject to agreed conditions and procedures.

Archives & Manuscripts Access

Those seeking access to archives must submit a formal application including:

  • Formal letter to the Director
  • Copy of curriculum vitae
  • At least two reference letters

Upon approval from the Ministry, researchers must pay applicable research fees and complete an admission form for the search room.

Research Fees

  • Local Malawi Students K300.00
  • Local Malawi Passport Holders K600.00
  • Foreign Passport Holders US$500.00

Library Access

Researchers seeking published sources (books, newspapers, periodicals) from the Library section can access materials free of charge with no formal application required.

Semi-Current Records

Researchers accessing records deposited by government departments or private organizations must provide written consent from the depositing agency.

Upcoming Projects & Developments

The National Archives of Malawi is committed to continuous improvement and expansion of its services. Key projects include:

  • Computerization of the archival system for improved access and management
  • Digitization of film collections to enhance preservation and accessibility
  • Publishing the Malawi National Bibliography online
  • Public awareness campaigns on the Archives' role in national development
  • Construction of a purpose-built archival facility (estimated cost: K35,000,000)
  • Intensification of records and literature surveys to consolidate the Archives' mandate

These ambitious projects reflect the Archives' vision to be a modern, accessible institution serving Malawi's information and research needs.

Strategic Partnerships

Since independence, the National Archives of Malawi has established itself as a premier information institution. However, the Archives recognizes the importance of collaboration with other key institutions.

The Archives works closely with:

  • University of Malawi Libraries
  • National Library Service

These partnerships ensure researchers have comprehensive access to information resources. The National Archives pledges to continue strengthening these valuable collaborations to better serve Malawi's research and information needs.
